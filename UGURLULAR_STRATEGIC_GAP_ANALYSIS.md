## 2. üèóÔ∏è Top 10 Structural Gaps (Ranked by Risk)

1.  **Lack of "Batch-Level" Energy Attribution:**
    *   *Risk:* You cannot claim "Low Carbon" for a specific premium yarn if you only have the factory's *monthly* electricity bill. EU CBAM requires specific emissions data.
2.  **Missing "Chemical Fingerprint" for Recycling:**
    *   *Risk:* SaXcell and Renewcell need to know the *exact* chemical composition (Titanium Dioxide, Antimony, Silicones) to prevent reactor failure. Current datasheets lack this.
3.  **Static "Brochureware" Mindset:**
    *   *Risk:* The current web presence is a marketing brochure. Zero@Ecosystem requires a *data platform* that brands log into daily.
4.  **Disconnect Between ERP & Shop Floor:**
    *   *Risk:* Manual data entry leads to errors and distrust. Machine IoT data (Spinning efficiency, kWh) must flow automatically into the DPP.
5.  **Invisible Supply Chain (Tier 2+):**
    *   *Risk:* "Organic" claims are unverifiable without Ginner and Farm level visibility. Blockchain or DNA tracer evidence will soon be mandatory.
6.  **Undefined "End-of-Life" Protocol:**
    *   *Risk:* Selling yarn without instructions on how to recycle it is no longer acceptable under ESPR (Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation).
7.  **Over-reliance on "Certificates" vs. "Data":**
    *   *Risk:* A PDF GOTS certificate is not a DPP. A DPP is a dynamic data set containing the certificate *plus* 50 other real-time metrics.
8.  **Inability to Calculate "Blend Impact":**
    *   *Risk:* Designers mix fibers. If your system can't calculate the CO2 of a 60/40 Blend dynamically, you lose the design-phase decision maker.
9.  **No API for Brands (IT Integration):**
    *   *Risk:* H&M and Inditex want to pull data directly into their PLM systems. They don't want to visit a website.
10. **Executive "Greenwashing" Blind Spot:**
    *   *Risk:* Believing that "having solar panels" makes the product sustainable, without measuring the actual *grid mix* usage during night shifts.
